Caring for Your Plus Size Corset – Cleaning & Storage Tips

A plus size corset is more than just a fashion statement—it’s an investment in your confidence, your style, and in many cases, your posture and waist training journey. But like any delicate garment, a corset requires proper care to keep it looking beautiful and functioning well over time. Whether you wear your corset daily or only for special occasions, learning the right way to clean, maintain, and store it is the key to ensuring it lasts for years.

Many people think corsets, especially steel-boned plus size corsets, can be thrown into the washing machine like any other piece of clothing. This is one of the biggest mistakes! Corsets are made of strong but delicate materials—such as steel boning, satin, mesh, brocade, or cotton—that need to be treated with care. By following proper corset care practices, you won’t just keep your garment clean; you’ll preserve its shape, durability, and comfort.

Why Corset Care Matters

A high-quality plus size corset is designed to support and shape your body. But if it’s not maintained correctly, it can lose its structure, develop rusting in the steel bones, or even break down at the seams. Proper care ensures:

  • Steel boning stays rust-free and supportive.
  • Fabric retains its smooth, beautiful finish.
  • Laces remain strong and don’t fray prematurely.
  • The corset continues to fit comfortably and effectively.

How to Clean Your Plus Size Corset

Unlike regular clothing, corsets should never be machine washed or tumble dried. Instead, stick to these steps:

  1. Spot Cleaning: If you notice a small stain, gently clean it using a damp cloth with mild detergent. Pat, don’t rub, to avoid damaging the fabric.
  2. Air Out After Wearing: Each time you take off your corset, hang it up in a well-ventilated area to allow moisture (like sweat) to evaporate.
  3. Hand Wash Occasionally: For more thorough cleaning, lightly hand wash the corset using cool water and gentle soap. Avoid soaking the steel boning for too long.
  4. Never Wring Out: Instead of twisting the corset to dry, gently press it between clean towels to absorb moisture.
  5. Air Dry Flat: Lay your corset flat to dry on a towel, away from direct sunlight or heat sources.

Storing Your Corset the Right Way

Storage is just as important as cleaning. Folding or storing your corset improperly can lead to creases, fabric wear, or bent steel bones. To keep your plus size corset safe:

  • Loosen the Laces: Never store your corset fully tightened. This helps the fabric and boning relax.
  • Store Flat or Hang: Lay the corset flat in a drawer, or hang it on a padded hanger to prevent creases.
  • Keep Away from Moisture: Store your corset in a dry place to prevent rusting of steel bones.
  • Use a Cotton Bag: If you travel, place your corset in a breathable cotton garment bag rather than plastic.

Extra Tips for Long-Lasting Corsets

- Rotate between multiple corsets if you waist train daily. - Avoid wearing lotions or oils directly under your corset to protect the fabric. - Always unlace carefully rather than pulling your corset off quickly. - Consider using a corset liner or light cotton top underneath to reduce sweat buildup.

By treating your corset with respect, you’ll enjoy its benefits for years to come. A well-maintained corset not only provides shaping and support but also continues to make you feel powerful and beautiful each time you wear it.
